About Us

Mission

The Family Engagement Program's mission is to empower families through strong community partnerships, economic development, healthy living, educational advancement, and physical activity. We create a supportive and inclusive environment where families can thrive and reach their full potential, ensuring a brighter future for all. By strengthening the family unit, we enhance the well-being of our communities, believing that resilient families are the foundation of thriving communities. Through our efforts, we are committed to building a better world, one family at a time.

Vision

The Family Engagement Program's vision is to create a world where every family has access to the resources and support they need to flourish. Our vision includes strong, empowered families leading their communities toward greater economic stability, educational excellence, and health and wellness. By fostering collaboration and shared responsibility, we envision communities where every family is valued and given opportunities to succeed, ensuring a sustainable and prosperous future for generations to come.

Goals

  • Increasing public awareness about the environmental issues affecting society now and in the future. Our program emphasizes youth - the leaders of tomorrow.
  • Collaborating with community educators to develop innovative hands-on learning projects in underserved schools focused on mitigating pollution and future climate-change impacts.
  • Reduce the effects of environmental pollution on EJ communities throughout the Pee Dee.
  • Using the environment as a tool to improve the academic performance of students in all core subject matters.
  • Integrate nature-based projects into youth-at-risk mentoring programs.
  • To build educational workforce pipelines leading to environmental careers.
  • Promote community resilience through environmental education and action programs.
  • Be a conduit for voicing disadvantaged community concerns to policy makers and government leaders.
  • Build community partnerships and coalitions to address the needs of disadvantaged communities in the Pee Dee.

History of the Environmental Awareness Center

Our most recent pilot project is the Environmental Awareness Center located at the Darlington Middle School in Darlington, SC. The Center’s goal is to improve student academic performance by using the environment as a unique, hands-on learning tool built in the school’s own backyard. For this collaborative project, the Families Engagement Program worked with the Darlington County School District and Clemson University to obtain grant support for the project. Families Engagement also assisted with the development of the educational materials and demonstrations at the Center. Started in 2023, the Environmental Awareness Program sprung from another successful pilot program started at the Middle School in 2020 called the Research Courtyard Program where all students participate in an outdoor research project specific to the science standards for his or her grade.

    Achievements

    Just completed in the Environmental Awareness Center is the sustainable garden project where students and the community learn about the physical and mental health benefits from both a good diet and outdoor exercise. Students also learn about using natural means of controlling unwanted pests around the yard and garden and how to build nontraditional floral gardens out of materials such as used tires.
